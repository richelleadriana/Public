### Post 1 - 3d Edited

Google just launched new communications about its approach to generative AI, covering the impact of AI on jobs, the environment, misinformation, and the other ways AI is reshaping society. (Link to full document in comments)

Hat tip to Marie Haynes for sharing and highlighting some key information that publishers and content creators have been demanding for a long time. (I took this screenshot with highlights from her post on X)

Two things stand out:

Google advises AI model developers to give website owners choice and control over whether their content is used in model development.

And most importantly:

Google is "exploring new types of partnership and value-exchange models. For instance, we are piloting novel ways to partner with websites whose content meaningfully contributes to the freshness and factuality of generative AI responses through the process of grounding."

I will certainly be watching this space closely. Personally, I believe *all* content creators whose work is used in AI training and/or grounding should be compensated according to how frequently their content is used.
